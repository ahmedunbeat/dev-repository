# Track Decision

## My Track

I have chosen the **[Backend]** track for the rest of the bootcamp.

## Three Signals that Pointed Me Here

1. **[Signal 1]**
Practising the Backend Fundamentals I, Exercise 1 (variable interpolation in PHP) and mini-project
(CLI student portal ledger) were tasks I could enjoy doing continously.

2. **[Signal 2]**
The entry difficulty is lower. I want to be employed, working even remotely and earning by next
year, and Backend gives me credible path to that.

3. **[Signal 3]**
Some job postings I found on LinkedIn wanted PHP Lavarel Systems and can work remotely. The bootcamp's
Backend track curriculum maps directly to some of the listings.

## The Runner-Up I Considered

I  also seriously considered **DevOps**. I ruled it out because Week 9 Mini-Project (System Diagnostics)
made it clear I need deeper knowledge and more resources such as MacOs, cloud account and other tools.
I would rather get good at Backend first and add the other after I'm earning with more experience.

## What I'm Nervous About

The OOP-heavy parts of PHP and Laravel systems. The many in-built syntax I observed in PHP such as classes, __construct, sprintf(), |, %s, %d, %f, ucword, this->, => etc. I plan to do extra reading on PHP OOP in coming weeks. I am also worried that most of the Job postings on Backend developer online were requesting for Python, Javascript, C#, Go and Java as required Backend Language at Mid-entry or Senior level.

## Commitment to Myself

By the end of Phase 2 (Month 6), I will have built and deployed three Laravel-backed projects on Github,
each with a working REST API  and a test suite. At least one of them will be a real tool I'd use myself and if possible start looking out for Backend developer PHP Laravel system job listings.
 
I'm signing this commitment to make it harder for me to back out when the works gets hard.
